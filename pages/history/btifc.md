### Summary

The British Troops in Foreign Countries (BTIFC) was the earliest organised form of British Scouting for troops stationed abroad. Established in 1909, just two years after Robert Baden-Powell founded the Scout Movement, BTIFC provided a structure for British military personnel overseas to continue their Scouting activities and maintain the British Scout tradition while serving away from home.

BTIFC played a crucial role in spreading Scouting beyond the United Kingdom and established the foundation for British Scouting's international presence. The organisation adapted Scouting programmes to the unique circumstances of military life abroad.

### Mini Timeline

**1909** — Founded as British Troops in Foreign Countries

**1909-1946** — Expanded alongside British military deployments worldwide

**1947** — BSG (British Scouts in Germany) created as a specialist branch

**1952** — <span class="badge bg-warning text-dark">Split</span> Reorganised into BGA (civilian expatriates) and BSG (military focus)
