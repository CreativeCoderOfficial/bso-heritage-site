### Summary

The Scout Association is the governing body for Scouting in the United Kingdom. Founded in 1908 following the experimental camp at Brownsea Island in 1907, it represents the original Scouting movement established by Robert Baden-Powell. The UK Scout Association serves as the parent organisation for British Scouting both domestically and abroad, providing programmes, training, and support for Scouts worldwide.

As the founding national Scout organisation, it established the patterns and traditions that would be adopted by British communities overseas, leading to the creation of various international branches including British Troops in Foreign Countries (BTIFC) and later British Groups Abroad (BGA).

### Key Milestones

**1907** — <span class="badge bg-success">Formation</span> Brownsea Island experimental camp

**1908** — <span class="badge bg-success">Founded</span> The Scout Association officially established

**1909** — First international branches formed (BTIFC)

**1910s-1920s** — Rapid expansion across the British Empire

**1967** — Creation of the Advance Party report leading to modern programme

**2002** — Introduction of the Scout Law and Promise review

**Present** — Continues to support Scouting in the UK and for British communities worldwide
