### Summary

The British Scouts in Germany (BSG) was a transitional organisation created in 1947 to serve British military personnel and their families stationed in post-war Germany. As part of the British Army of the Rhine (BAOR) presence, BSG provided Scouting opportunities for children of service personnel in the British occupation zone.

This was a relatively short-lived organisation that later evolved into the BSWE (British Scouting in Western Europe) structure, adapting to the changing military landscape in Germany and across Western Europe.
