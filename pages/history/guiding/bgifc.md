### Summary

British Guides in Foreign Countries (BGIFC) was the international branch of Girlguiding UK, established in 1911 to provide Guiding opportunities for British girls and women living overseas. For over a century, BGIFC supported Guiding across international British communities, maintaining the traditions and values of UK Guiding while adapting to local circumstances.

BGIFC operated in parallel to the Scouting organisations for boys, providing a complete Guiding programme for British expatriate communities worldwide. In 2017, the organisation was restructured and renamed as British Guides Overseas (BGO).

### Historical Context

BGIFC played a crucial role in maintaining British cultural identity and community cohesion among expatriate families. The organisation provided:

- Continuity of Guiding programme for mobile families
- Social connections for British girls in foreign countries
- Leadership development opportunities for women overseas
- Support for military families and diplomatic communities
