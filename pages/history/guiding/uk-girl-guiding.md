### Summary

Girlguiding UK is the governing body for Guiding in the United Kingdom. Established in 1910, just two years after Scouting, it was founded by Robert Baden-Powell and his sister Agnes Baden-Powell to provide similar opportunities for girls and young women. The organisation has grown to become the largest girl-only youth organisation in the UK.

Girlguiding UK established international branches to support British girls and women living abroad, most notably British Guides in Foreign Countries (BGIFC), which later evolved into British Guides Overseas (BGO).

### Key Milestones

**1909** — Robert Baden-Powell forms the Girl Guides

**1910** — <span class="badge bg-success">Founded</span> GirlGuiding UK officially established

**1911** — First international branches formed (BGIFC)

**1912** — <span class="badge bg-info text-dark">Rename</span> Becomes Girl Guides Association

**1917** — Brownies section introduced for younger girls

**1943** — Guides rename to Girlguiding UK

**1992** — Introduction of the Duke of Edinburgh's Award for Guides

**Present** — Continues to support Guiding in the UK and for British communities worldwide
