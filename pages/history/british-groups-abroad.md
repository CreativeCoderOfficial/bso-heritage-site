## British Groups Abroad: The Founding Era (1909-2013)

### Overview

The British Groups Abroad (BGA) represented the earliest organized form of British Scouting outside the United Kingdom. Established in 1909, just two years after the founding of the Scout Movement, BGA provided a structure for British expatriates and military personnel stationed overseas to continue their Scouting activities and maintain connections to the British Scout tradition.

This period saw the formation of countless Scout Groups across Europe, the Commonwealth, and beyond, as British families and military communities established local units that followed UK Scouting programmes while adapting to their host countries' unique circumstances.

### Historical Significance

**Key Developments:**
- **1909**: First British Scout Groups formed abroad by expatriate families
- **1920s-1930s**: Growth alongside British military presence worldwide
- **Post-WWII**: Expansion as British communities rebuilt overseas
- **1960s-1970s**: Formalized structures for international British Scouting

**Legacy:**
The BGA era established the foundation for British Scouting's international presence, creating a network that would eventually evolve into the modern British Scouting Overseas organization. Many Groups founded during this period continue to operate today under the BSO name.

### Notable Groups from This Era

While a complete directory is available in our [Group Directory](/browse.html?filter=type%3AGroup), some historically significant Groups from the BGA period include:

- **1st Berlin British Scouts** - One of the earliest continuous Groups in Europe
- **Hong Kong British Scouts** - Serving the British community in Asia
- **Brussels British Scouts** - Long-standing presence in the heart of Europe
- **Gibraltar Scouts** - Unique British Overseas Territory Group
