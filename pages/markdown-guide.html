Markdown Guide

A simple guide to Markdown formatting for BSO Heritage articles.

Writing in Markdown

Markdown is a simple way of writing formatted text using ordinary characters. The website converts it into a styled page automatically. You do not need to know any code.

Write everything below the second --- line.

Headings

You type
## About This Group

### Early Years

#### 1950s Leadership
Result on the page

About This Group

Early Years

1950s Leadership

Always start your article headings with ## (two hashes). The page title is already shown as the biggest heading — starting at ## keeps everything in the right order.

Bold and italic

You type
**This is bold text**

*This is italic text*

***Bold and italic***
Result

This is bold text

This is italic text

Bold and italic

Lists

You type
- First item
- Second item
- Third item

1. Step one
2. Step two
3. Step three
Result
  • First item
  • Second item
  • Third item
  1. Step one
  2. Step two
  3. Step three

Links

You type
[Link text here](https://example.com)
Result
Link text here

Blockquotes — for quotes from documents or people

## About the 1st Brussels Group

The **1st Brussels Group** was established in 1954 to serve the
growing British community in the Belgian capital.

## History

### Early Years (1954–1967)

The group initially met in the British Embassy compound.

- 1954 — Group formally registered with British Groups Abroad
- 1958 — First camp held in the Ardennes
- 1963 — Reached 40 members

> "Our Group gave children the stability of Scouting
> while their families navigated life abroad."
>
> — Former Group Scout Leader, 2021
