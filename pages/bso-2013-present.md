### The Formation of BSO

British Scouting Overseas (BSO) was officially established in 2013, bringing together the former British Groups Abroad (BGA) and British Scouting in Western Europe (BSWE) under a single, unified organization. This merger created a more cohesive structure for delivering British Scouting to young people living outside the United Kingdom.

The formation of BSO reflected the changing nature of British expatriate communities worldwide and the need for a more flexible, responsive organization that could support Scouting in diverse international contexts.

### Modern BSO Structure

Today, BSO operates as an independent charity recognized by UK Scouts, serving young people in countries where access to British Scouting would otherwise be limited. The organization:

- **Directly supports** Groups in over 20 countries
- **Provides programmes** aligned with UK Scouting while respecting local laws and cultures
- **Offers training** and resources for adult volunteers
- **Maintains connections** with the wider international Scouting community

### Key Developments (2013-Present)

**2013-2015: Establishment Phase**
- Merger of BGA and BSWE organizations
- Development of new governance structure
- Creation of unified branding and identity

**2016-2020: Growth and Expansion**
- Increase in member countries
- Development of digital resources
- Enhanced international cooperation

**2021-Present: Modernization**
- Adoption of new UK Scouting programmes
- Enhanced online presence and digital tools
- Strengthened relationships with national Scout organizations

### The BSO Experience Today

Young people in BSO enjoy the same programme as their peers in the UK, with adaptations to reflect their international context. Activities include:

- Weekly meetings following UK Scout programmes
- International camps and events
- Community service projects
- Cultural exchange opportunities
- Badge work and skills development

### Get Involved

BSO is always welcoming new members and adult volunteers. Whether you're a British expatriate family, an international family interested in British Scouting, or a local resident wanting to join, there's a place for you in BSO.
